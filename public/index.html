Our Vision

Self-governing boys that are collaborating, playing their role in society, living life by the values of Torah—where spirituality is tangible, interacting with physicality, sanctifying God's name.

Boys who make logical and brave decisions, who set goals and achieve them. Who wake up excited to learn—not because they are told to, but because they have discovered that learning is one of the deepest pleasures of being alive.

Our Mission

To provide a safe place—a safe, calm, respectful environment with collaborative problem solving and coaching as opposed to bribery, coercion, and harsh disciplinary measures.

We work together with parents with transparency and view ourselves as partners in your child's growth.

  • Respect Every child's dignity is non-negotiable.
  • Safety A calm environment where emotional regulation can flourish.
  • Collaboration Problem-solving together, not top-down instruction.
  • Coaching Guiding self-discovery, not imposing solutions.

Our Philosophy

We do not bribe children to learn. No sticker charts. No external reward systems. We believe that bribing a child to read is the fastest way to teach them to hate reading.

Instead, we build on proven modalities—Montessori, Waldorf, and Acton Academy—frameworks that trust children, protect their autonomy, and develop their inner drive.

The SODAS Method

A structured thinking protocol we teach every student:

S See the Situation
O Options What paths forward?
D Disadvantages & Advantages
A Act Choose and own it

This is not just a problem-solving tool. It is a life skill. A child who can run SODAS on a conflict, a difficult problem, or a moral dilemma is a child who is becoming a thinker—and a mentsch.

Our Long-Term Goal

To establish a legal, structured school in Beit Shemesh honoring the stages of development of our students and inspiring them to become the heroes that they're meant to be—with autonomous Torah learning and real-world responsibility.

This starts now, with a small group of families who believe it is possible.
